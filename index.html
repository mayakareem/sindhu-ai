3
Separate Task Types
Not all work is the same. I break every task into categories to determine the right level of AI intervention. This prevents over-engineering and ensures the lightest effective intervention is applied - no one wants a $500K agent doing a $5 job.
Automate
Repetitive, rules-based tasks. No judgment needed. Examples: data entry, status updates, standard reports, notification routing.
Copilot
Judgment-based but assistable. Human decides, AI drafts/suggests. Examples: email drafts, meeting prep, research summaries, proposal generation.
Agent
Multi-step action with system access. Operates independently within guardrails. Examples: triage workflows, cross-system data sync, follow-up sequences.
Human-Owned
High-risk decisions, relationship-sensitive moments, strategic judgment calls. AI supports context, but the human decides and acts.

30-60-90 Day Plan

A structured ramp-up: listen first, design second, deliver third.

Illustrative plan - timelines and priorities will adapt to the CEO's vision and Lucidya's immediate needs.

Phase 1
Listen, Map, Earn Trust
The first 30 days are not about changing anything. They're about understanding the business deeply enough to know what to change and earning the credibility to do it.
Week 1-2: Immerse & Understand
  • Meet every function lead 1:1 - Sales, CS, Marketing, Product, Engineering, HR, Finance, Legal, Ops
  • Shadow real work: sit in on sales calls, watch CS handle tickets, observe how approvals flow
  • Meet key clients to understand their experience of Lucidya's operations from the outside
  • Understand the tech stack: what tools are used, what's integrated, what's manual
  • Map the informal power structure - who are the go-to people, who unblocks things
Week 3-4: Map & Baseline
  • Document the top 10-15 highest-volume workflows across all functions end-to-end
  • For each: map steps, systems, owners, handoffs, approvals, recurring bottlenecks
  • Establish baseline metrics: cycle time, wait time, rework rate, quality risk, cost of delay
  • Identify the "pain points everyone knows about but nobody has fixed"
